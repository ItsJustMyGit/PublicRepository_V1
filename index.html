CHADWICK BOSEMAN

American Actor, Playwright and Director

Through His Life and Career

  • Early Life
  • Born - November 29, 1976 Anderson, South Carolina, U.S.
  • Parents - the son of Carolyn and Leroy Boseman, both African-American. His mother was a nurse and his father worked at a textile factory, and managed an upholstery business.
  • In his youth, Boseman practiced martial arts and continued this training as an adult. As a child, he wanted to become an architect
  • Education
  • Highschool - Graduated from T. L. Hanna High School in 1995 where he played on the basketball team.
  • In his junior year, he wrote his first play, Crossroads, and staged it at the school after a classmate was shot and killed. He was recruited to play basketball at college but chose the arts instead
  • College - Attended college at Howard University in Washington, D.C. and graduated in 2000 with a Bachelor of Fine Arts in directing
  • He attended the Oxford Summer Program of the British American Drama Academy at Balliol College, Oxford in 1998
